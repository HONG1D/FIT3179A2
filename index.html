Malaysia Domestic Tourism Analysis (2017 - 2023)

Malaysia, a multicultural nation rich in heritage and natural. It remains one of Southeast Asia’s most popular travel destinations.
This visualization explores Malaysia’s domestic tourism trends through three key perspectives. The National Domestic Tourism Trend
section highlights yearly changes in total visitors and average expenditure per trip. The State Performance Matrix compares visitor's
growth rate across states by years. Lastly, the State Economic Overview visualizes tourism receipts and visitor distribution across states.
Together, these charts provide a comprehensive view of how tourism activity and performance vary across time and regions in Malaysia.

Domestic Tourism Trend

This section tracks Malaysia’s overall Domestic tourism performance from 2017 to 2023. The visualization highlights:

  • All three variables (Total Domestic Visitors, Total Expenditure, Average Expenditure per Trip) have a positive correlation.
  • 2017–2019: Malaysia’s domestic tourism grew steadily, indicating healthy expansion consistent with Malaysia’s strong economy.
  • 2020–2021: Pandemic collapse, visitor numbers fell ~73%, expenditure dropped ~82%, and average expenditure per trip declined ~18%.
  • 2022–2023: Domestic tourism recovery, although total visitors and expenditure have not yet returned to 2019’s peak, people are spending more per trip, suggesting a shift toward higher-value leisure travel.

State Performance Matrix

This section compares the performance of each state across multiple years. The visualization highlights:

  • Kuala Lumpur and Selangor consistently record the darkest marks on the heatmap, indicating they attract the highest and most stable number of domestic visitors across all years.
  • Labuan, Perlis, and Putrajaya remain among the states with the lowest visitor numbers due to their smaller population and tourism base.
  • The overall growth trend for most states follows a clear pattern: positive(2018–2019) → negative(2020–2021) → strong positive(2022) → moderate positive(2023).
  • 2017–2019: Most states experienced moderate positive growth reflecting a stable domestic tourism market.
  • 2020–2021: All states suffered a sharp decline in both visitor numbers and growth rates due to the COVID-19 pandemic and MCO.
  • 2022: A major rebound occurred following the easing of restrictions and government campaigns such as "Cuti-Cuti Malaysia".
  • 2023: Tourism activity stabilized, maintaining a healthy growth level and signaling a full domestic recovery phase.

State Economic Overview

This section explores the tourism receipts and number of visitors across Malaysian states. The visualization highlights:

  • Kuala Lumpur and Selangor are main hubs of domestic tourism as they dominate both color and circle size.
  • Labuan, Putrajaya, and Perlis display smaller circles and lighter colors indicating that they attract fewer visitors and generate relatively lower tourism receipts.
  • East Malaysian states such as Sabah and Sarawak demonstrate strong tourism receipts relative to the number of visitors, reflecting longer stays or higher average spending per trip.
  • Domestic tourism reflects a strong positive relationship between total visitors and total receipts meaning that destinations with more tourists generally generate higher revenue.
  • Kuala Lumpur appears as a noticeable outlier in the scatter plot, having an exceptionally high number of domestic visitors compared to other states.
