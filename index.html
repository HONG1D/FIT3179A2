Malaysia Domestic Tourism Analysis (2017 - 2023)

Malaysia, a multicultural nation rich in heritage and natural. It remains one of Southeast Asia’s most popular travel destinations. This visualization explores Malaysia’s domestic tourism trends through three key perspectives (National Domestic Tourism Trend section, State Performance Matrix and State Economic Overview). Together, these charts provide a comprehensive view of how tourism activity and performance vary across time and regions in Malaysia.

Domestic Tourism Trend

This section highlights yearly changes in total visitors and average expenditure per trip.

Total Domestic Visitors and Expenditure Over Time

From 2017 to 2019, Malaysia’s domestic tourism expanded steadily. This reflects a healthy economy and growing interest in local travel. However, between 2020 and 2021, the pandemic caused a sharp collapse. Visitor numbers fell by about 73%. Total expenditure dropped by 82%. Average spending per trip declined by 18%. In 2022 and 2023, the sector began to recover. Although visitor numbers and total expenditure have yet to reach pre-pandemic peaks. Malaysians are spending more per trip suggesting a shift toward higher-value leisure experiences.

State Performance Matrix

This section compares visitor's growth rate across states by years.

Domestic Visitor Distribution for States by Year

Kuala Lumpur and Selangor consistently record the darkest marks on the heatmap. This reflects that they are Malaysia’s primary urban and economic hubs. In contrast, Labuan, Perlis, and Putrajaya attract the fewest visitors. This might due to limited tourism infrastructure.

Full Overview Diverging Bar Chart

The overall growth trend for most states follows a clear pattern: positive(2018–2019) → negative(2020–2021) → strong positive(2022) → moderate positive(2023).

Filtered Diverging Bar Chart

From 2017 to 2019, most states experienced steady growth in domestic tourism. A sharp decline occurred in 2020 and 2021 due to COVID-19 pandemic and MCO. A strong rebound emerged in 2022 as restrictions eased and “Cuti-Cuti Malaysia Program” encouraged travel. 2023 marked a period of stabilization and signaling a full Post Pandemic Recovery phase.

State Economic Overview

This section visualizes tourism receipts and visitor distribution across states. Kuala Lumpur and Selangor serve as the main hubs of domestic tourism. They dominate both visitor numbers and tourism receipts. In contrast, Labuan, Putrajaya, and Perlis attract fewer visitors and generate relatively lower receipts. East Malaysian states of Sabah and Sarawak show strong tourism receipts relative to visitor numbers. This suggest longer stays or higher average spending per trip.
